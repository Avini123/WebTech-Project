Ivory-Billed Woodpecker

The Ivory-Billed Woodpecker is native to the forests of Southern United States and Cuba. Habitat destruction and hunting have devastated the species's population. In the 21th century, sightings have been reported. However, due to its lack of confirmed sightings, the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service have proposed for a declaration for the species to be extinct. The final decision is due by September 2022. Despite this, some people still hope for the Ivory-Billed Woodpecker to reappear. Sadly, it is now a functionally extinct bird.
