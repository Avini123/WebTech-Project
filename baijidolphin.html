Baiji Dolphin

The Baiji Dolphin is a dolphin species that is thought to have been the first of its species to be driven to extinction due to human activity. By local fishermen and boatmen, it is regarded as the goddess of protection. This kind of dolphin saw its declining population during China's industrialization. The last known living Baiji Dolphin died in 2002, causing the World Wildlife Reservation to call for the preservation of any of the specie's habitat in case it can be located or revived. In 2007, there was a Baiji Dolphin spoted in the Yangtze. However, this is not enough for the species to recover its population. Thus, the dolphin is considered a functionally extinct animal.
