North Atlantic Right Whale

The North Atlantic Right Whale has a docile nature and stays close to the coast. Due to their high blubber content, they were hunted to near extinction by whalers. Presently, there are less than 366 of these whales left in the wild. Fishing nets and vessel strikes are the two greatest threats to the species's recovery.
